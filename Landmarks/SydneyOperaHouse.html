Sydney Opera House

Introduction

The Sydney Opera House is an art performing venue located in Sydney, New South Wales, Australia. It was designed by a Danish architect Jørn Utzon. The Sydney Opera House is a landmark known all over the world. The opera house hosts more than 1500 performances per year with more than 1.2 million people in attendance. The house features a variety of shows including theatre, classical music, opera, cabaret, dance and more.

Design

It is engineered in the expressionist design with concrete shells covering the top serving as a roof. If you were to look up at the roof close up, you can see a chevron pattern consisting of two colours: glossy white and matte cream.
