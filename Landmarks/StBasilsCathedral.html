St. Basil’s Cathedral

Introduction

St. Basil's Cathedral is located on the southern end of the Red Square in Moscow, Russia. It has many different colors, shapes, and patterns designed in a style that is unique to the Russian Culture. It is close to the world-known Kremlin which is also around the Red Square.

History

The cathedral was built by Ivan Vasilyevich who was nicknamed "Ivan the Terrible". The construction of the cathedral was ordered in 1554 to honor a military victory. Ivan's armies defeated the independent Tatar khanates of Kazan and Astrakhan in the 1550s. Today the cathedral serves as a landmark in Moscow, Russia.
