Statue of Liberty

Introduction

The Statue of Liberty is a monument located in Liberty Island, New York, United States of America. It is built by the French engineer and architect, Gustav Eiffel. Gustav Eiffel was well known for being one of the architects for the world-known Eiffel Tower. The Statue of liberty is a symbol of freedom and democracy. which is told in its other name “Liberty Enlightening the World” which is a translation of the French name “La Liberté éclairant le monde”. It is a gift of friendship from France dedicated on October 28, 1886.

Meaning

Over time, the statue's meaning has become more than just freedom but it has become a symbol for greeting immigrants, wishing that they would find hope and opportunities in America. The Lady on the pedestal is wearing roman attire similar to the Roman goddess Libertas who embodied liberty. The crown is similar to what we know today as a halo and the spikes resembled the crowns wore by the sun gods like the roman Appollo who is the god of the sun and Greek Helios who personified the sun. The lady's right foot is raised as if she was to move and bring freedom to everyone. The fire on the torch is a symbol of knowledge and light which were major inventions lead to civilization.
