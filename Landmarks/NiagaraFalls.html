Niagara Falls

Introduction

Niagara Falls is a combined name of three waterfalls located at the border of Ontario, Canada and New York, United States of America. The largest of the three falls is “Horseshoe Falls”, the second largest waterfall is “American Falls”, and the smallest is “Bridal Veil Falls”.

Horseshoe Falls

Horseshoe Falls is the largest out of the three falls. This is because of about 90% of the water that flows down from the Niagara River passes through Horseshoe Falls. Horseshoe Falls source from Lake Erie and flows to Lake Ontario. It has a 90° drop for 51 meters to the Lower Niagara River.

Hydroelectricity

Horseshoe Falls is currently used for hydroelectric power generation. It first began as a very small hydroelectric generation plant of only 2200 kilowatt in the year 1893. The power station was named Niagara Falls & River Railway Power House in 1893. The station was then named Sir Adam Beck Hydroelectric Generating Stations in 1950 which was as large as a 2 million kilowatt power plant. There consists of 2 generating stations in the Sir Adam Beck Hydroelectric Generating Stations. Sir Adam Beck Hydroelectric Generating Station I and Sir Adam Beck Hydroelectric Generating Station II.
