Niagara Falls

Introduction

Niagara Falls is the combined name of three waterfalls located at the border of Canada and United States of America. The largest of the three falls is Horseshoe Falls, the second largest waterfall is American Falls, and the smallest is Bridal Veil Falls.

Horseshoe Falls

Horseshoe Falls is the largest out of the three falls. This is because of about 90% of the water that flows down from the Niagara River passes through Horseshoe Falls. The fall source from Lake Erie and flows to Lake Ontario. It has a 90° drop, 51 meters down to the Lower Niagara River.

Hydroelectricity

Horseshoe Falls is currently used to generate hydroelectric power. It first began as a very small hydroelectric generation plant of only 2200 kilowatts in the year 1893. The power station was named Niagara Falls and later River Railway Power House in 1893. The station was then named Sir Adam Beck Hydroelectric Generating Stations in 1950 which was then became a 2 million kilowatt power plant. There consists of 2 generating stations in the Sir Adam Beck Hydroelectric Generating Stations: Sir Adam Beck Hydroelectric Generating Station I and Sir Adam Beck Hydroelectric Generating Station II.
