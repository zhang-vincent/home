Great Wall of China

Introduction

The Great Wall of China was built over a long period of time. The building of the wall started as early as 7th century BC and the majority of the wall was built during the Ming Dynasty which was from 1368â€“1644. Many segments of the wall are parallel to each other in the northern section of China. The total distance of the Great Wall is 21196.18 kilometers and it took 2 500 years to complete.

History

In the beginning, it was used as a fortification against provinces but later, more segments of the wall were built in northern China to prevent invasions coming in that direction.

Construction Period

Zhou Dynasty (770-221-BC)
- A Qi State duke builds segments to prevent invasions from other states
Qin Dynasty (221-207 BC)
- More segments are built due to the Qin Emperor wanting to secure the Northern borders
Han Dynasty (206-220 AD)
- The wall is expanded to protect the Silk Road Trade
Ming Dynasty (1368-1644)
- Most of the wall is rebuilt and restored
Qing Dynasty(1644-1911)
- Continuation of construction was made
Modern Time
- In 1957, it once again goes through a major reconstruction
